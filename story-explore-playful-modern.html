Your index

The genre of Metroidvania games comes from Nintendo's Super Metroid (1994) and Konami's Castlevania: Symphony of the Night (1997). These games defined characteristics of the genre, such as a focus on exploring a map non-linearly, acquiring items, weapons, tools, or abilities, and tight integration of story and level design.

While most Metroidvanias are side-scrolling platformers, other types of games have qualified for the title by highlighting their Metroidvania characteristics. Regardless, Metroidvanias are finely tuned to balance story, character, level, and interaction in masterful ways.
